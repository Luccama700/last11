# RATINGS-LADDER.md — calibration sheet for Lucca

Every squad in `src/engine/data/squads-v2.json` with its **top-3 rated players**, so you
can red-pen the whole scale in one sitting. **47 squads / 833 players / 20 nations,
1930→2026** (every tournament year covered except the WWII-cancelled 1942/1946). Ratings
are per-tournament snapshots on your DECISIONS.md anchors.

**Fixed anchors (binding, already applied):** Pelé 1970 = **97** (unique ceiling) ·
Maradona 1986 / Messi 2014* / R9 Ronaldo 2002 / Messi 2022 = **96** · Messi 2026 = **92**
· Gabriel Magalhães 2026 = **89**. *(Messi 2014 not yet in data.)*
Band meaning: 96 = inner-circle GOAT peak · 95 = all-time-great peak · 91–94 = a top
team's talisman · 88–90 = very good starter · 85–87 = solid starter · 82–84 = squad
regular · ≤81 = depth. Tight top, high floor, defenders not punished.

## Cross-year arcs (the same player across tournaments — the snapshot principle)

**70 players** now have 2+ snapshots. The sharpest calibration surface — the same name,
rated at his age each year:

- **Pelé:** 1958: **93** (17-year-old breakout) → 1962: **90** (injured after match two,
  limited minutes) → 1970: **97** (peak, the ceiling) — a three-point arc: rise, dip
  through injury, then the greatest-ever peak. The dataset's headline story.
- **Ronaldo (R9):** 1994: **80** (unused 17-yo) → 2002: **96** (peak)
- **Cafu:** 1994: **84** (young rotation) → 2002: **91** (peak captain)
- **Passarella:** 1978: **87** (title-winning captain, 24yo) → 1986: **85** (elder
  statesman, squad player behind Maradona's team)
- **Giuseppe Meazza:** 1934: **91** (peak, Italy's first title) → 1938: **89** (veteran
  captain, ceding the spotlight to young Piola)
- **Johan Neeskens:** 1974: **89** → 1978: **87** · **Ruud Krol:** 1974: **86** →
  1978: **85** · **Rensenbrink/Rep/Jansen/Suurbier** all step down 1–4 pts, veterans of
  both Dutch finals.
- **Garrincha:** 1958: **89** (breakout, second fiddle to Pelé) → 1962: **92** (his
  Golden Ball tournament, arguably his career peak) — the one arc that goes UP more than
  Pelé's dip that same year, reflecting he carried the team in Pelé's absence.
- **Messi:** 2022: **96** (Golden Ball) → 2026: **92** (veteran)
- **Mbappé:** 2018: **90** (teen) → 2026: **93** (peak)
- **Modrić:** 2018: **91** (Ballon d'Or peak) → 2026: **86** (age-40 veteran)
- **Cristiano Ronaldo:** 2006: **87** (young winger) → 2026: **85** (age-41 veteran)
- **Dembélé:** 2018: **83** → 2026: **91** (2025 Ballon d'Or form)
- **Kane:** 2018: **88** → 2026: **91** · **Lautaro:** 2022: 84 → 2026: 89 ·
  **Enzo Fernández:** 2022: 85 → 2026: 88 · **J. Álvarez:** 2022: 87 → 2026: 90

## Where I'd expect the red pen (open calibration questions)

1. **96-tier company.** Maradona 86 / R9 2002 / Messi 22 all sit at 96; **Puskás 54**
   is the one 95 I placed adjacent to that tier (the "greatest team never to win it,"
   losing finalist). Should he join 96, or does a runner-up cap at 95 regardless of
   individual quality?
2. **Cannavaro 92 as Italy 2006's top man**, above Buffon 91 and every attacker — is a
   defender-led ceiling right, or should a GK (Buffon) top a defensive champion?
3. **2026 ceiling = Mbappé 93.** Nobody current hits 94+. Correct that the modern game
   has no 95, or should Mbappé/Yamal push higher?
4. **Spain 2026 has three at 90+ (Rodri 92, Yamal 92, Pedri 90)** — too stacked?
5. **Modrić −5 (91→86) over 8 years and CR7 −2 (87→85)** — are the veteran decays the
   right slope?
6. **Messi 2022 = 96 vs Messi 2026 = 92** (−4 in 4 yrs). Too steep / too shallow?
7. **Fourth-place sides (Portugal 06, England 18, Morocco 22, South Korea 02) top out at
   82–88.** Right that a semifinalist has no 90, or are the individual talismen of those
   runs (Hong Myung-bo 85, Kane 88) underrated relative to eventual champions?
8. **Historical depth floor is 75–78** (3rd keepers, minnow bench, or squads with no
   verified shirt numbers). Deep enough spread, or compress the bottom?
9. **Pre-1970 legends without a later snapshot** (Puskás, Beckenbauer 74, Matthäus 90,
   Rossi 82, Meazza 34/38) sit in the 88–95 range on reputation + single-tournament
   dominance alone. Fair, or does the genre need a different anchor treatment for
   players without a second career-peak comparison?
10. **Pelé's three-point arc (93 → 90 → 97)** — does the 1962 injury dip (−3 from his
    '58 breakout) undersell him, given he still won that tournament as a squad member?
11. **Zico 93 as Brazil 1982's ceiling**, above the tournament's actual champions
    (Rossi 91, Italy). Is "greatest team never to win it" earning the higher individual
    rating than the actual winner defensible, or should elimination cap the ceiling?
12. **Garrincha's 1962 peak (92) edges past his more decorated 1958 self (89)** — is
    "Golden Ball, carried the team" the right lens for a snapshot rating, even in a
    tournament his side won more narrowly than in '58?
13. **New-nation snapshots** (Milla 87, James Rodríguez 91, Hong Myung-bo 85) sit
    comfortably in the talisman/very-good-starter bands on single-tournament brilliance —
    consistent with the "quality not fame" principle, or should a smaller football
    nation's ceiling be capped lower regardless of individual dominance?

Change any number and I re-rate the squad + surrounding anchors to keep proportions sane.
